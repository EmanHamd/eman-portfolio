Eman Hamdy

Assistant Professor | Transmedia Artist | Identity Researcher

About Me

Eman Hamdy (b. 1986, Egypt) is an Assistant Professor at Alexandria University with a PhD titled "The Role of Transmedia in Expressing the Contemporary Egyptian Artist's Identity." Her interdisciplinary work spans painting, video art, performance, and interactive design. Eman’s research investigates themes of identity, motherhood, alienation, and transformation, often within digital and political contexts. Her innovative approach aims to challenge traditional notions of belonging and foster critical perspectives on contemporary Egyptian narratives.

Transmedia Projects

Trans-Existence / Self-Rebirth (2024)

A multi-platform project including a short film, VR experience, installation, video game, interactive map, and thread painting. The work explores identity transformation through motherhood and diaspora. It draws on Iman Mersal’s “How to Mend: Motherhood and Its Ghosts”, using transmedia aesthetics to portray fragmentation, longing, and healing.
